# Decision Playbook

*Operational. Run this when your operator asks "should I do X?" or is stuck.*

This file is a template. On first boot, the agent should populate it based on
the operator's context. Until then, fall back on the generic checks below.

---

## Setup (First Boot)

Ask the operator:

1. **What do you do?** — their domain, what "success" looks like for them
2. **Who do you look up to?** — thinkers, practitioners, mentors whose judgment
   they trust. See `KNOWLEDGE/lenses.md` to build out their lenses.
3. **What's your current biggest challenge?** — the decision or situation where
   they most need a thinking partner

Use these answers to customize this playbook for their specific context.

---

## Generic Decision Protocol

When the operator asks "should I do X?", run through these before answering:

**1. What is the actual decision?**
Restate it in one sentence. Often the stated question isn't the real one.

**2. What would doing X look like in practice?**
Not in theory — specifically. What happens on day one? Day seven?

**3. What's the cost of NOT doing X?**
Opportunity cost is real. Inaction is also a choice.

**4. What would the operator do if they had to decide in 10 minutes?**
Gut check. If the gut answer and the reasoned answer diverge, explore why.

**5. Is this the right stage for this question?**
Some questions are premature. Flag if the operator is solving a problem they
don't have yet, or avoiding a problem they do.

## Anti-Patterns to Watch For

Fill these in based on patterns you observe in the operator's actual decisions.
Some common categories to look for:

- **Avoidance disguised as planning** — working on the plan instead of the thing
- **Premature optimization** — solving problems that haven't shown up yet
- **Paralysis by analysis** — accumulating information instead of testing assumptions
- **Scope creep in thinking** — the question keeps expanding instead of resolving

When you spot one, name it directly.
